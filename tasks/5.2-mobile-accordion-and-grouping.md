# 5.2 — Mobile Accordion and Grouping

## What

Refine accordion and grouped result behavior for mobile screens.

## Why

The desktop interaction patterns need smaller-screen tuning for touch targets,
collapsed density, and grouped-child readability.

## Changes

- Tune accordion hit areas and spacing for touch.
- Ensure grouped cards remain readable when collapsed.
- Ensure child rows do not become visually ambiguous on mobile.
- Preserve Phase 1 and Phase 2 semantics; this task is refinement, not logic
  redesign.

## Completion Conditions

- Accordion controls are comfortably tappable on mobile.
- Grouped cards remain understandable in collapsed and expanded states.
- No overlap or clipping makes child rows unreadable.

## How to Test

### Browser/manual test: `mobile_accordion_touch_targets_are_comfortable`

Expected:

- Result headers are easy to tap.
- Expanding/collapsing does not require precision clicking.

### Browser/manual test: `mobile_grouped_cards_preserve_child_readability`

Expected:

- Top-scoring child remains identifiable.
- Context children remain distinguishable from matched children.

## Depends On

- 1.2 accordion results.
- 2.2 grouped accordion cards.

## Notes

- Test this on at least one narrow mobile viewport and one mid-size tablet
  viewport before marking complete.
